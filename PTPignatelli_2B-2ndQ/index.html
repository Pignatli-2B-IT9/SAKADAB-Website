An Introduction

Our Partner Institution

What is SAKADAB?

Sakadab also known as Samahan ng may iba't ibang Kakayahan sa Dabaw is an organization that helps people with disabilities. This organization provides livelihood for them. The institution exists in order to help the advocacy of the organization as well as the people behind it. It provides a training center for persons with disabilities so that they may be productive members of the community.

An Introduction

All About It

Organizer

The organization that founded Sakdab is Gawad Kalinga Community Development Foundation, Inc. (GK). Their goals are to end poverty for 5 million families, by first restoring the dignity of the poor, they also aim to promote a 'culture of caring and sharing' by encouraging collaboration among all sectors of society in the effort to get communities out of poverty.

History

Many people have been in a difficult situation trying to find a home for themselves and their families as a result of court demolition orders in Davao City, as well as for people with disabilities and who are physically challenged. On July 15, 2010, these people who were struggling because of their disabilities and lose of homes envisioned having their own homes and way of life, and with the help of Mayor Inday Sarah Duterte and the City Government, Mr. Oscar Hidalgo, Gawad Kalinga, and the Lions Club International, the City Government awarded them Aland in Los Amigos, Tugbok, Davao City. The area is now known as SAKADAB-GK Village.

Beneficiaries

The SAKADAB also known as Samahan ng may iba't ibang Kakayahan sa Dabaw is a community of 27 who are faced by physical challenges lives in Davao City, these people came together from Our Lady Victory - Training Center to work together to achieve the common goal of increasing the quality of living and becoming self-reliant. They have no specific programs or services which they offer. Instead they provide each other with spiritual, moral, and financial support to live a more meaningful life.Some of the activities they have are farming, baking, handicraft, woodcraft, and welding.
