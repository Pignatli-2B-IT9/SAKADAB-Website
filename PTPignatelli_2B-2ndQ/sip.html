SIP

The effectiveness of crushed eggshells as compost to the height of mung bean plant

Problem Statement What is the effect of crushed eggshells to the height of the mung bean plants?
Hypothesis If eggshells are used as compost for mung bean plants, then they will grow taller than the untreated mung bean plants.
Independent Variable The use of crushed eggshells as compost
Dependent Varible Height of the mung bean plants
Constant or Controlled Variables Amount of water, Amount of Sunlight, Location of the experiment, Amount of soil, Type of water, Amount of seeds in each pot (all set-ups), Type of soil, and Type of plant (Vigna radiata)

Significance of Study

The value we receive from composting contributes to getting rid of the amount of trash in the environment of our communities through reusing the biodegradable trash that is thrown away by other people. This improves the quality of soil, increasing moisture retention, allowing for roots to build strong networks and to attain nutrients, and controlling pests and diseases that affect plants. Composting doesn't require any fertilizer, thus lessening the use of chemical fertilizers. During composting, we use natural and organic things like trash, plant, and animal waste so we don't cause pollution. Whereas chemical fertilizers contain harmful chemicals that cause soil and water pollution. Through boosting the defense and nutritional gains of a plant, it becomes advantageous for the health of the soil and the food produced by the plant. The use of composting provides an abundant amount of value to communities and their environment. According to EPA (United States Environmental Protection Agency), (2022, February 12), https://www.epa.gov/sustainable-management-food/reducing-impact-wasted-food-feeding-soil-and-composting#:~:text=Compost%20retains%20a%20large%20volume,fuels%2C%20herbicides%2C%20and%20pesticides , compost retains a large volume of water, thus helping to prevent/reduce erosion, reduce runoff, and establish vegetation. Compost improves downstream water quality by retaining pollutants such as heavy metals, nitrogen, phosphorus, oil and grease, fuels, herbicides, and pesticides. Because of composting we require less soil conditioners, chemical fertilizers, and pesticides. All these things contribute to an over all healthier environment for our communities. Our partner institution, SAKADAB, can benefit from this by using composting to grow and sell plants, for example, from our experiment about Vigna Radiata plant (Mung Bean Plant). Through the use of compost, they can grow healthy and high quality Mung Beans efficiently. With these Mung Beans they can create delicious meals that can be sold and promoted through online platforms, signs, posters, etc. From the money the gain from selling these meals they can invest and provide for the members in the SAKADAB community.


Experimental Set-up

1 Pot , 165 grams of crushed egg shells, 60ml water , 1 seed , Spade , 6kg of loam soil (3 replicates)

Controlled Set-up
